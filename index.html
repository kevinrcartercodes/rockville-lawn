Products I use

Pre-emergent (late March / early April)

Dimension (dithiopyr) — the only common pre-emergent with a short-enough residual (~12 weeks) to allow fall overseeding. Prodiamine (Barricade) blocks September germination for 4–6 months and is off the table.

Starter fertilizer (September, at seeding)

Jonathan Green Veri-Green Starter 12-18-8 — the 18% phosphate is legal at seeding/renovation under Maryland's new-lawn exemption. Do not use "Love Your Lawn – Love Your Soil" as a starter; it's a gypsum soil conditioner, not a starter fertilizer.

Maintenance fertilizer (Oct + second Nov app)

Any MD-legal turf fertilizer with ≥20% slow-release N and no phosphorus. Apply at 0.9 lb N / 1,000 sq ft (no more than 0.7 lb of it soluble). Scotts Turf Builder and Jonathan Green Green-Up both qualify.

Seed

Jonathan Green Black Beauty Original or Black Beauty Delmarva — both 100% tall fescue blends formulated for the Mid-Atlantic. Overseed Sept 1 – Oct 1 for best results.

Maryland law (don't get fined)

  • Blackout: no lawn fertilizer application Nov 16 – Mar 1. Last legal day is Nov 15.
  • Per-application cap: ≤ 0.9 lb total nitrogen / 1,000 sq ft, of which ≤ 0.7 lb may be water-soluble.
  • Annual cap: follow UMD's rate table. Enhanced-efficiency products: up to 2.5 lb N/1,000 sq ft/year, with ≤ 0.7 lb released in any month.
  • Slow-release: all turf fertilizer sold in MD must contain ≥ 20% slow-release nitrogen.
  • Phosphorus: prohibited on established lawns unless a soil test shows deficiency. Permitted at establishment, patching, or renovation (starter fertilizer).
  • Setbacks: 15 ft from any waterway (10 ft with a drop spreader or rotary spreader with deflector shield). Sweep fertilizer off impervious surfaces. No application when heavy rain is forecast, or when ground is frozen or saturated.
